# Market Research & Validation

## The Problem
Smallholder farmers operate on razor-thin margins. They lack access to:
1. **Real-time Data**: Reliance on generic weather forecasts rather than micro-climate data.
2. **Actionable Insights**: Difficulty interpreting complex agricultural data into tangible actions.
3. **Accessibility**: Existing AgTech solutions require high literacy and constant internet access.

## Target Demographic
* **Primary Users**: Small to medium-scale farmers in developing nations.
* **Secondary Users**: Agricultural cooperatives and large-scale farming operations looking for granular field data.

## Market Size
* The global precision agriculture market is projected to reach **$20+ billion by 2030** ([Grand View Research, 2023](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/precision-agriculture-market)).
* Smallholder farmers produce roughly **30% of the world's food** ([UN Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), 2021](https://www.fao.org/news/story/en/item/1395127/icode/)), representing a massive, underserved segment for affordable AgTech.

## User Pain Points Addressed
* **Over-fertilization**: Causes soil degradation and wastes money. TerraSense prescribes exact dosages based on NPK levels.
* **Water Scarcity**: Inefficient irrigation. TerraSense uses soil moisture data to optimize watering schedules.
* **Crop Disease**: Delayed detection leads to massive yield loss. AI-driven anomaly detection provides early warnings.
